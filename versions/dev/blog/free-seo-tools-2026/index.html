10 Free SEO Tools Every Website Owner Should Use in 2026

Published May 22, 2026 · ~12 min read

Let's face it: SEO tool subscriptions add up fast. Ahrefs is $129/month. SEMrush is $140/month. Even Moz Pro, the "budget" option, starts at $49/month. If you're running a small business website, a personal blog, or a side project, dropping hundreds of dollars a month on SEO software probably isn't in the budget.

The good news? You don't have to. There's a growing ecosystem of completely free SEO tools that cover the essentials: site audits, keyword research, content optimization, technical checks, and more. In 2026, the gap between free and paid SEO tools is narrower than ever — especially if you know where to look.

Below are 10 free SEO tools that every website owner should have bookmarked. Every single one is free to use right now — no trial periods, no credit card forms, no "upgrade to see results" walls.

#1 · Site Audit

SEO Textools — Self-Evolving Site Audit

This one's our own tool, and we're putting it first because it genuinely does something most free audit tools don't: it learns from your usage. Unlike static checklists that score every site the same way, SEO Textools adapts its scoring based on your industry, site size, and the historical patterns it observes as you run audits over time.

Run a free site audit and it will check your meta tags, headings, images, links, page speed indicators, schema markup, mobile usability signals, and more. Best for: Indie site owners who want ongoing technical SEO monitoring without a paid subscription. Try it free — no signup, no page limits.

#2 · Content Optimization
2

Keyword Density Analyzer

Google has come a long way since the days of keyword stuffing, but keyword density still matters — not as a strict ranking signal, but as a readability and relevance metric. If your target keyword appears too few times, search engines might not understand what your page is about. If it appears too often, your content reads like a spammy sales pitch.

The Keyword Density Analyzer at SEO Textools calculates the exact frequency and density of every keyword in your content. It highlights single-word and multi-word phrases, shows you what percentage of your total word count each term represents, and helps you strike the right balance between optimization and natural writing.

Pro tip: Aim for 1–3% density for your primary keyword. If you're above 5%, your content probably needs editing. Below 0.5%, consider whether you've actually established topical relevance.

#3 · SERP Preview
3

SERP Preview Tool

Your title tag and meta description are the first things searchers see — and they're your only chance to earn that click. A well-optimized title and description can improve your click-through rate by 20–30%, even if you're ranking in position five or six.

The SERP Preview Tool shows you exactly how your page will appear in Google search results. It simulates the title tag truncation (titles longer than about 60 characters get cut off), the meta description display (around 155–160 characters), and the URL breadcrumb. You can tweak and re-preview until everything looks perfect before you publish.

Why this matters: Google's display algorithms aren't consistent — sometimes they rewrite your title, sometimes they pull description text from the body content. Previewing helps you catch display issues before they cost you clicks.

#4 · Search Console
4

Google Search Console

Google Search Console (GSC) is the most powerful free SEO tool in existence — and yet, a surprising number of site owners never set it up. GSC gives you direct data from Google about how your site performs in search: which queries bring traffic, which pages rank highest, what your average position is, and how many impressions and clicks you're getting.

Beyond performance data, GSC surfaces critical issues: indexing errors, Core Web Vitals problems, mobile usability bugs, manual actions, and security issues (malware, hacked content). It also lets you submit sitemaps and request manual indexing of new pages.

Pro tip: Connect GSC with your free SEO audit tool to combine Google's first-party data with your own technical crawl analysis. Use the Sitemap Parser to validate your sitemap before submitting it to GSC.

#5 · Technical SEO
5

Sitemap Parser

Your XML sitemap is the roadmap you give to search engines. If it's broken, outdated, or missing important pages, Googlebot might never discover your best content. The Sitemap Parser lets you paste your sitemap URL and instantly see every URL, its last-modified date, change frequency, and priority.

Common issues this tool catches: missing or broken URLs, inconsistent priority settings, pages that should be excluded (tag pages, filter pages, duplicate content), and empty sitemaps that serve no purpose. A clean sitemap is one of the fastest technical SEO wins you can get.

#6 · Structured Data
6

Schema Markup Generator

Structured data (schema markup) helps search engines understand your content and can unlock rich results like star ratings, FAQ accordions, recipe cards, and event listings. Pages with rich results tend to earn higher click-through rates because they occupy more visual space in the SERP.

The Schema Markup Generator creates JSON-LD structured data for common schema types — Article, FAQ, Product, LocalBusiness, Organization, and more. Just fill in your details and copy the generated code into your page's <head>. No coding experience needed.

#7 · Performance
7

Page Size Analyzer

Page size directly affects load speed, and load speed affects both user experience and rankings. Google's Core Web Vitals update made page performance a confirmed ranking factor, and bloated pages are a common — and easily fixable — cause of poor scores.

The Page Size Analyzer checks the total size of any web page and breaks it down by resource type: HTML, CSS, JavaScript, images, fonts, and third-party scripts. If one category is dominating your page weight, you know exactly where to optimize. Aim for under 2 MB total, with images ideally making up no more than half of that.

#8 · Accessibility & SEO
8

Alt Text Checker

Alt text serves two audiences: visually impaired users who rely on screen readers, and search engine crawlers trying to understand your images. Neglecting alt text means losing traffic from Google Image Search — which can be a significant traffic source for many sites.

The Alt Text Checker scans a page and reports every image's alt attribute status: present and descriptive, present but empty, missing entirely, or keyword-stuffed. It's a quick way to spot accessibility and SEO gaps across your site.

#9 · Core Web Vitals
9

Google PageSpeed Insights

Google's PageSpeed Insights is the official tool for measuring Core Web Vitals — LCP (Largest Contentful Paint), INP (Interaction to Next Paint, which replaced FID in 2024), and CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift). Google uses these metrics as ranking signals, so improving them directly impacts your search visibility.

PageSpeed Insights gives you separate scores for mobile and desktop, along with specific recommendations: "Optimize images," "Eliminate render-blocking resources," "Reduce unused JavaScript," and more. Each suggestion comes with an estimated time savings so you can prioritize fixes by impact.

Pro tip: Use the Page Size Analyzer alongside PageSpeed Insights — the former gives you raw resource sizes, while the latter tells you how those sizes affect real-user performance.

#10 · Content Stats
10

Word Counter

Word count seems trivial, but it's a foundational SEO metric. Google tends to rank comprehensive content higher — studies consistently show that the average first-page result is around 1,500–2,500 words. That said, longer isn't always better; every paragraph should add value.

The Word Counter gives you a clean, instant word and character count for any text. It's useful for keeping meta descriptions under 160 characters, title tags under 60 characters, and blog posts in that sweet spot of thoroughness without fluff.

Bonus: How to Build a Free SEO Workflow

Having individual tools is great, but combining them into a repeatable workflow is better. Here's a free weekly SEO routine that uses all of the tools above:

  1. Monday (Audit): Run a full site audit on SEO Textools. Note any new errors or warnings.
  2. Tuesday (Content): Use the Keyword Density Analyzer on your latest drafts. Adjust keyword usage to hit 1–3% density.
  3. Wednesday (SERP): Preview your titles and descriptions with the SERP Preview Tool before scheduling posts.
  4. Thursday (Technical): Check PageSpeed Insights. Run the Page Size Analyzer on slow pages. Optimize images.
  5. Friday (Cleanup): Validate your sitemap, check alt text across your site, and review GSC for any new issues.
